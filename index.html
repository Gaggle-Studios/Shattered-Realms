How To Play

Goal

Reduce your opponent's HP to 0. The Archmage can also win by reaching 50 Insight.

Choose a Hero

The Warden — Nature and beasts. Resilient minions, buffs, and board control.

The Shadow — Assassins and goblins. Sacrifice synergies, stealth, and burst damage.

The Archmage — Wizards and control. Spells, counters, and an alternate win condition via Insight. Unspent mana at the end of your opponent's turn converts to Insight.

Turn Structure

  1. Upkeep — Gain +1 max mana (starts at 5), refill mana, untap all minions, trigger Inspire and Regenerate effects.
  2. Draft — Pick 1 of 3 cards from your personal shop to add to your hand. You may spend 1 mana to reroll the shop. (Skipped for the first player on turn 1.)
  3. Action — Play cards from your hand and/or initiate combat (once per turn, not on turn 1).
  4. End Turn — Temporary effects expire. Draw cards up to your hand limit (default 5).

Card Types

Minion — A creature with Attack and HP. Enters the battlefield and can attack or block during combat.

Instant — A spell you can play at any time, including during your opponent's turn. Goes on the stack.

Sorcery — A spell you can only play during your own Action phase when the stack is empty.

Enchantment — A persistent effect that stays on the field, providing auras or triggered abilities. Exiled when destroyed.

Upgrade — A permanent buff attached to a friendly minion, granting stats and/or keywords. Exiled after being played.

Curse — A debuff that targets an enemy minion, stripping keywords and applying negative effects. Exiled after being played.

Combat

Combat works very similarly to Magic: The Gathering. The active player taps minions as attackers, the defender assigns blockers, then damage resolves simultaneously. First Strike, Trample, Deathtouch, and other familiar keywords work as you'd expect.

Key differences from MTG:

  • Combat is not available on the very first turn of the game.
  • There is no separate main phase after combat — you return to the same Action phase.
  • Minions do not replenish their health at the start of your next turn.

The Stack & Priority

The stack and priority system works exactly like Magic: The Gathering. When you play a card, your opponent can respond with an Instant, and so on. The stack resolves last-in, first-out. If you have no playable Instants, priority is automatically passed.

Tips

  • Use the View Deck button on the hero select screen to browse all cards before playing.
  • Hover over any card to see a detailed tooltip.
  • Draft strategically — shop cards are generally more powerful than starter cards.
  • Keep mana open on your opponent's turn if you have Instants to respond with.
